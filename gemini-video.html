Gemini + Video

Integrate Gemini into Drive Videos to decrease consumption time and increase productivity

See my process | Check it out in Drive

Overview

Gemini + Video brings AI-powered capabilities directly into the video viewing experience in Google Drive. The goal was to help users extract key information from videos faster, reducing consumption time while increasing productivity.

UX Lead
  • Led the integration of Gemini AI features into the Drive video player
  • Designed the interaction model for AI-assisted video consumption
  • Collaborated with Gemini, Drive, and Meet teams to ensure seamless cross-product experience
Impact
  • Decreased video consumption time for key tasks
  • Increased user productivity when reviewing video content
  • Successfully shipped AI-powered video features across Drive
Context

As video content proliferates across Google Workspace, users need smarter ways to consume and extract value from videos. Gemini integration enables users to ask questions about video content, get summaries, and navigate to key moments—all without watching the entire video.

Problem
  • Users spend excessive time watching full videos to find specific information
  • No way to quickly understand video content without watching
  • Cross-referencing video content with other documents is manual and time-consuming
Goal

Enable users to interact with video content through natural language, making video consumption as efficient as reading a document.

Approach

I explored multiple integration points for Gemini within the video experience, considering the side panel, inline overlays, and contextual triggers.

Design Explorations

We iterated on how Gemini should surface within the video player—testing side panel placement, prompt interfaces, and response formatting to find the right balance between utility and viewing experience.

Findings
  • Users valued the ability to ask questions about video content
  • Side panel integration felt natural and non-disruptive
  • Summary features were the most-used capability
  • Users wanted the ability to jump to specific moments mentioned in AI responses
